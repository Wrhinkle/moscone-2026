# ZS Associates

> A 40+ year-old management/analytics consulting firm now selling agentic AI strategy and its ZAIDYN AI platform into life sciences and commercial functions.

- **Category:** enterprise-adopters
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 1983, Evanston, IL, by Kellogg School professors Andris (Andy) Zoltners and Prabhakant (Prabha) Sinha (Verified)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.zs.com

## What they do
ZS is a management consulting and technology firm (~13,500–21,000+ employees depending on source/date, 40+ global offices) originating from sales-force sizing/territory-alignment models built on early personal computers in the early 1980s. Today it sells agentic AI strategy consulting plus its own platform, ZAIDYN, which unifies data, analytics, and workflows across commercial, medical, and patient-facing teams for life-sciences clients. As an engineer you'd engage ZS less for infrastructure and more for domain-specific AI workflow design/implementation (pharma commercial ops, market access, R&D analytics) layered on top of client data stacks.

## Founders & funding
Founders Andy Zoltners and Prabha Sinha, both Kellogg professors (Verified — multiple obituary/company sources); Zoltners died in 2026. Privately held; no venture funding found (funding databases show no disclosed VC rounds — Not found in public sources for any equity raise). Estimated revenue ~$4.2B (Reported, Zippia).

## Evidence of real-world use
Named a Leader in Forrester Wave for customer analytics services (agentic AI expertise cited) and in the 2025 Everest Group PEAK Matrix for life-sciences AI/analytics (Reported — vendor press). Case study with Dataiku on using AI agents to reduce risk and improve model reliability (Reported, single source).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
A large-scale example of a traditional consulting firm packaging agentic AI into a vertical SaaS platform (ZAIDYN) — relevant to this repo's enterprise-adoption and vertical-agent research areas, and a useful contrast to venture-backed agent-infra vendors elsewhere in this landscape.
