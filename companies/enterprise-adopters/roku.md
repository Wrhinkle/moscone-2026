# Roku

> The streaming-TV platform company, now applying AI/ML across content discovery, ad serving, and ad creative generation at consumer scale.

- **Category:** enterprise-adopters
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2002, Los Gatos, CA (Verified — public company, NASDAQ: ROKU)
- **Website:** https://www.roku.com/ · https://advertising.roku.com/

## What they do
Roku isn't an AI vendor — it's a large-scale enterprise adopter: a streaming hardware/OS platform (Roku devices, Roku OS, The Roku Channel) that runs machine learning across two core surfaces. First, content discovery/recommendation: the home-screen UI and search are ML-driven to surface content predicted to keep users engaged. Second, advertising: Roku's Ads Manager and ad-serving stack use ML for supply/demand matching and targeting, and it has rolled out AI-generated TV ad creative for small/mid-size advertisers plus "contextual AI" that scans show/movie content to place brand ads next to matching plot moments in real time. Its ML/ads engineering teams (per public job postings) work on marketplace intelligence, demand optimization, and generative ad tooling.

**Founders & funding:** Founded by Anthony Wood (Verified); publicly traded since 2017, no private funding rounds to report.

**Evidence of real-world use:** In-production at consumer scale (tens of millions of active accounts); Roku management has publicly framed AI investment as a growth driver on earnings calls (AdExchanger coverage).

**Relevance to agentic AI engineering:** A case study in production ML/ads infrastructure and generative content pipelines at large scale — relevant to this repo's enterprise-adoption and recommendation/ads-infra threads rather than to agent frameworks directly.
