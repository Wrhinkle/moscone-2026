# Indeed

> The world's largest job-search platform, now shipping agentic features on both sides of its marketplace — job seekers and recruiters/employers.

- **Category:** enterprise-adopters
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2004, Austin/Stamford (Verified — Paul Forster and Rony Kahan)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.indeed.com/ (no public GitHub of note)

## What they do
Indeed is the incumbent job-search/ATS-adjacent marketplace, now layering conversational agents on its core search product rather than replacing it. **Career Scout** is a consumer-facing agent that interviews a job seeker about background/goals, then surfaces matched postings, builds resumes, and runs interview practice. **Talent Scout** is a recruiter-facing conversational agent that ingests job descriptions, screener questions, and hiring-manager notes to surface and rank candidates inside existing ATS integrations. **Sourcing Assistant** is a newer agentic feature that proactively finds and engages candidates for open reqs. For an engineer, Indeed here is an example of a large, high-traffic incumbent retrofitting agent UX onto a decades-old two-sided marketplace rather than a startup building agent infra from scratch.

## Founders & one-line funding note
Founded 2004 by Paul Forster and Rony Kahan on ~$5M raised (Union Square Ventures, Allen & Co.) (Verified). Acquired by Japan's Recruit Holdings in 2012 for a reported ~$1B, operating since as an independent unit within Recruit's HR Technology division (Verified).

## Evidence of real-world use
Not a startup pitch — Indeed already has enormous existing usage as the default job board for much of the US/global labor market. On the new agent features specifically: Indeed reports applicants sourced via Sourcing Assistant were "2.9x more likely to be hired" and hired ~6 days faster on average than other channels (Reported, company press release). Talent Scout also ships via a Salesforce Agentforce integration case study (Reported).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
A concrete large-scale case study of applying LLM agents to a real two-sided marketplace with hard business metrics (hire rate, time-to-hire) rather than a demo — useful reference for anyone building or evaluating "agent bolted onto existing enterprise product" patterns, and for back-office/HR-adjacent automation comparisons elsewhere in this repo.
