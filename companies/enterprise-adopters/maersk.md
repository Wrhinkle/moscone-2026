# Maersk

> The world's largest integrated container shipping and logistics operator, now retrofitting its global operations with predictive and generative AI.

- **Category:** enterprise-adopters
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 1904, Copenhagen, Denmark (Verified — as the Steamship Company Svendborg, by Peter Mærsk Møller and A.P. Møller)
- **Website:** https://www.maersk.com

## What they do
Maersk runs container vessels, terminals, and end-to-end supply-chain logistics at global scale — the kind of operation an AI engineer would touch via demand-forecasting models, route/fuel optimization, and increasingly a generative-AI layer over customer service and quoting. Its "Maersk Quotation AI Assist" and a GenAI customer-service system pull data from multiple internal systems to draft responses to shipment/delivery inquiries, which a human operator reviews and approves — a human-in-the-loop pattern rather than full autonomy. For 2026 Maersk states plans to move beyond predictive analytics into generative AI for planning and procurement, and to commercialize internal tools (e.g., Trade & Tariff Studio) as customer-facing products.

- **Founders/funding:** Not a startup — publicly traded (CPH: MAERSK), majority-controlled by the Møller family via A.P. Moller Holding; no VC funding applicable. (Verified)

## Evidence of real-world use
Maersk publicly reports ~20% operational-efficiency gains and >$300M in claimed annual savings from AI-driven forecasting/routing (Reported — company/press figures, not independently audited). The GenAI customer-service assistant and Quotation AI Assist are described as live, in-production tools handling real customer inquiry volume (Reported).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
A concrete enterprise-adopter case study for human-in-the-loop agent design, multi-system data integration for RAG-style assist tools, and the gap between predictive-AI maturity and emerging agentic/generative deployment at industrial scale.
