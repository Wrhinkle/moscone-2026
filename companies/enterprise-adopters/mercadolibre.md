# MercadoLibre

> Latin America's largest e-commerce and fintech platform (NASDAQ: MELI), applying LLMs across search, payments, and internal developer tooling at scale.

- **Category:** enterprise-adopters
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 1999, Buenos Aires, Argentina (Verified — by Marcos Galperin with Stanford MBA colleagues, inspired by eBay)
- **Website:** https://www.mercadolibre.com

## What they do
MercadoLibre operates marketplace, payments (Mercado Pago), logistics, and advertising businesses across 18+ Latin American countries — an engineer's-eye view is a large-scale e-commerce/fintech stack now layering LLMs on top: vector-embedding-based semantic search (with Grupo Boticario) to handle the ~50% of queries keyword search handles poorly, an in-house LLM orchestration system called "Verdi" for rapid prompt/experiment iteration, an internal LLM-powered documentation assistant for proprietary tech-stack questions, and MCP/agentic-IDE tooling applied to the Mercado Pago codebase. Its accessibility team also built a Jira+GitHub+AI-agent pipeline to automate ticket review.

- **Founders/funding:** Founded by Marcos Galperin (Stanford MBA, Wharton undergrad) and Stanford classmates; IPO'd on Nasdaq in 2007, raising $289M (Verified). No longer a funding-stage company — publicly traded, market cap in the tens of billions.

## Evidence of real-world use
Multiple named, published engineering case studies (not just marketing): the Boticario semantic-search deployment, the Verdi orchestration system, MCP-based Mercado Pago tooling, and Amplitude-reported internal usage (70 users, 126 AI agents built, 71% completion rate on value-generating tasks) — solid signal of genuine internal adoption (Reported, self-published engineering blog + vendor case study).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Strong reference for RAG/semantic search at scale, internal developer-productivity agents, and MCP adoption inside a large non-AI-native enterprise — relevant to this repo's memory/RAG and agent-orchestration research areas.
