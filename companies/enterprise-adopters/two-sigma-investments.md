# Two Sigma Investments

> A ~$60B quant hedge fund that treats data engineering and, increasingly, AI/LLM tooling as core infrastructure for investment research and trading.

- **Category:** enterprise-adopters
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2001, New York City (Verified — Wikipedia, company site)
- **Website:** https://www.twosigma.com/

## What they do
Two Sigma is a systematic/quantitative investment manager: it ingests data from 10,000+ sources, cleans/labels it at scale, and runs statistical and ML models to drive trading and asset-management decisions. Its public writing describes a shift from "deploying models" to embedding AI as an operating layer across engineering workflows — internal tools becoming "company aware" of Two Sigma's own platforms, research pipelines, and incident processes — plus standardizing data infra (e.g., BigQuery, CI/CD'd data transforms).

## Founders & funding
Founded by John Overdeck and David Siegel (ex-D.E. Shaw), with Mark Pickard, and seed capital from Paul Tudor Jones (Verified). Privately held, not VC-funded; co-CEOs Carter Lyons and Scott Hoffman took over from Overdeck/Siegel in Sept. 2024 (Verified).

## Evidence of real-world use / relevance
Not a vendor — an enterprise adopter integrating frontier LLMs into a large-scale, data-intensive engineering org; relevant as a case study in applying agentic/AI tooling to internal developer and research productivity rather than as a product to evaluate.
