# Uber

> Global ride-hailing/delivery platform whose internal ML platform, Michelangelo, has been extended into a full LLMOps and agent-building stack used company-wide.

- **Category:** enterprise-adopters
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2009, San Francisco (Verified)
- **Website:** https://www.uber.com/

## What they do
Michelangelo is Uber's end-to-end internal ML platform, now extended with LLMOps: fine-tuning data prep, prompt engineering, LLM fine-tuning/eval, deployment, and production monitoring, plus a Model Catalog exposing both third-party APIs (GPT-4, PaLM) and in-house-hosted open models (e.g., Llama 2). Uber's "GenAI Gateway" is a unified proxy layer giving internal teams consistent access to multiple LLM vendors and Uber-hosted models. Uber has also stated plans for an "agent studio" for building, evaluating, deploying, and managing agentic systems internally.

## Founders/funding
Not applicable here — Uber is a public company (NYSE: UBER); the relevant unit is internal engineering, not a funded product.

## Evidence of real-world use
100% of Uber's business-critical ML use cases, including GenAI features like LLM-based customer-service bots, run on Michelangelo (Verified — Uber engineering blog).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
A concrete large-scale case study in LLM gateway design, model routing/catalog patterns, and the move from ML platform to agent-ops tooling — directly useful reference architecture for teams building internal agent platforms.
