# YouTube

> The world's largest video platform (Google/Alphabet), now rebuilding its recommendation, dubbing, and creation stack around Gemini-based AI agents.

- **Category:** enterprise-adopters
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2005, San Bruno, CA; acquired by Google in 2006 for ~$1.65B (Verified)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.youtube.com

## What they do
YouTube isn't an AI vendor here — it's listed (AIEWF Supporting Partners tier) as an enterprise adopter shipping AI agents into a product used by billions. In 2026 it rewired its core recommendation engine around Gemini (Jan 2026, its biggest ranking change since watch-time replaced view-count in 2012), rolled out Gemini-driven AI dubbing with visual lip-sync across ~20 languages, and uses Gemini for comment moderation/categorization, age-verification signals, and flagging AI "slop" and deepfakes. It also ships consumer-facing generative tools (Shorts likeness/generation features, AI creation tools used daily by 1M+ channels as of Dec 2025).

## Evidence of real-world use
Verified via Google Cloud docs and press: Gemini-based recommendation engine live Jan 14, 2026; Gemini-powered dubbing/lip-sync shipped Sept 2025; 1M+ channels/day used AI creation tools (Dec 2025, Reported). This is production-scale deployment, not a pilot.

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Concrete large-scale case study of LLM-in-the-recommendation-loop, content moderation agents, and generative media pipelines at consumer scale — relevant to evals/safety (moderation), multimodal generation, and SDLC-for-ML research areas in this repo.
