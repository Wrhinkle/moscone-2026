# Reelful

> An AI video app that turns a user's camera roll into a fully edited, narrated short-form "reel" in about 10 minutes.

- **Category:** consumer-ai
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2025-2026, San Francisco area (Reported)
- **Website:** https://www.reelful.app/

## What they do
Reelful is a consumer iOS app that automates the short-form video editing pipeline: a user hands it a camera roll of raw photos/clips, and an agentic editing system selects footage, sequences it, adds narration/music, and outputs a polished Reel-style video — collapsing what's normally a manual editing workflow down to roughly 10 minutes end-to-end. It's built on Convex.dev for real-time backend infrastructure (state sync, media processing pipeline), which the founder has spoken about publicly as core to shipping fast. Monetized via iOS subscriptions (weekly/annual) plus consumable video-generation credits.

**Founders & funding:** Founded by Kate Deyneka, a former Snap machine-learning engineer, who built and launched the app in about six months; one source (Stealth Startup Spy) reports she raised $18M+ by 2026, but this is single-sourced and unconfirmed by a primary filing — treat as **Reported**, not Verified.

**Evidence of real-world use:** Live on the App Store with a public pricing page; press coverage (Fenado AI, Stealth Startup Spy newsletter) and founder-driven social/YouTube promotion, but no named enterprise customers (it's a consumer app) or independent usage metrics found.

**Relevance to agentic AI engineering:** A concrete example of agentic media-editing pipelines (multi-step tool orchestration over video/audio assets) and of Convex as an agent-friendly real-time backend — relevant to this repo's agent-orchestration and consumer-ai threads.
