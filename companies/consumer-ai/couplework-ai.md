# CoupleWork AI

> Consumer mobile app: an AI relationship coach ("Maxine") for couples, built on clinical therapy frameworks rather than open-ended chatbot conversation.

- **Category:** consumer-ai
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** Not found in public sources (app live on iOS App Store and Google Play as of 2026)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://couplework.ai — no public GitHub (closed-source consumer app)

## What they do
CoupleWork is a voice-and-text app centered on "Maxine," an AI coach trained on structured, evidence-based couples-therapy frameworks (the Gottman Method and Emotionally Focused Therapy) rather than generic conversational chat. It runs guided weekly ~45-minute sessions plus shorter ad-hoc check-ins, assessments, and follow-up exercises for both partners under one subscription ($24.95/mo). The company explicitly positions this as coaching, not therapy — Maxine does not diagnose or treat clinical conditions, which is a meaningful product/legal distinction from telehealth mental-health apps.

## Founders & funding
Co-founded by Clay Cockrell (25+ years as a psychotherapist) and Tony Fabrikant (20+ years in fintech) (Reported, company "About" page — single-source). No funding rounds, investors, or valuation found in public sources; treat as bootstrapped/undisclosed.

## Evidence of real-world use
Live, paying-customer product on both major app stores (iOS App Store, Google Play) with an active content/marketing site — real usage signal for a small consumer app, though no user counts, retention, or case-study data were found (Unverified beyond app-store presence).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Marginal direct relevance to agent-building infra; useful as a case study in narrow, guardrailed persona design (a single well-specified voice/text coaching agent constrained to a clinical framework, with an explicit non-diagnosis boundary) rather than open-ended chat.
