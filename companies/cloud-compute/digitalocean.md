# DigitalOcean

> Cloud infrastructure provider for developers and small-to-mid businesses, now pushing an "AI-native cloud" stack (GPUs, inference, and managed agents) on top of its simple VPS/Kubernetes core.

- **Category:** cloud-compute
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** silver
- **Founded:** 2011, New York (Verified — Wikipedia, Crunchbase)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.digitalocean.com

## What they do
DigitalOcean is a public-cloud provider (NYSE: DOCN) known for simple, predictably-priced Droplets (VMs), managed Kubernetes, and object storage aimed at developers who find AWS/GCP too complex. In 2025–2026 it layered a GenAI Platform on top: managed access to third-party foundation models (Anthropic Claude, Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek, GPT-4) with RAG, function routing, agent customization, and guardrails so teams can build and deploy agents without deep ML expertise. At Deploy 2026 it announced an "AI-Native Cloud" spanning five layers — infrastructure, core cloud, inference, data, and managed agents — targeting the "inference era" workload pattern (many small, bursty GPU jobs vs. long-lived training runs).

## Founders & origins / Funding
Founded by Ben Uretsky, Moisey Uretsky, Jeff Carr, Alec Hartman, and Mitch Wainer in 2011 (Verified). Raised ~$174M total (Seed $3.2M 2013 via IA Ventures; Series A $37.2M 2014 a16z; Series B $83M 2015 Access Industries; $50M 2020) before IPO'ing on NYSE in March 2021 at $47/share (Verified — Wikipedia, Crunchbase, TechCrunch coverage).

## Evidence of real-world use
600,000+ customers overall (mostly on the traditional cloud side). Named early GenAI/AI-Native Cloud adopters: Autonoma, Higgsfield AI, Hippocratic AI, ISMG, Bright Data, and LawVo (Reported — DigitalOcean blog/press, not independently confirmed).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
A lower-cost, lower-complexity alternative to hyperscalers for teams running agent infrastructure, inference endpoints, and RAG pipelines — relevant to this repo's cloud-compute and agent-deployment tracks alongside Vercel, Modal, and RunPod.
