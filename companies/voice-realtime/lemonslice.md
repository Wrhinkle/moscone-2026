# LemonSlice

> A real-time AI avatar model that turns a single still image into a live, talking video call — a video layer for voice agents.

- **Category:** voice-realtime
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2024 (Verified — TechCrunch)
- **Website:** https://lemonslice.com

## What they do
Lemon Slice's core model (Lemon Slice-2) takes one image — a headshot, illustrated character, or painting — and animates it into a live, low-latency, two-way conversational video call, effectively giving any voice-agent pipeline a synced, real-time face instead of a static avatar or audio-only interface. It's aimed at developers building interactive video agents (companions, brand mascots, virtual presenters) who want lip-synced, expressive video generated on the fly rather than pre-rendered.

## Founders & funding
Founded by Lina Colucci, Sidney Primas, and Andrew Weitz (PhDs from MIT/Harvard/Stanford/Duke). Raised $10.5M in seed funding from Matrix Partners and Y Combinator, with angel participation from Dropbox CTO Arash Ferdowsi, Twitch CEO Emmett Shear, and The Chainsmokers (Verified — TechCrunch, PR Newswire).

## Evidence of real-world use
Public launch announcement (Dec 2025) with named investors and a working demo model; team size reported at 8. Independent customer case studies not yet found — evidence so far is launch coverage rather than deployed-customer proof (Unverified for production customer use).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Sits squarely in the voice/realtime layer of the agent stack: pairs with speech-to-speech and TTS pipelines to give conversational agents a real-time visual presence, relevant to multimodal/voice-agent tracks at AIEWF.
