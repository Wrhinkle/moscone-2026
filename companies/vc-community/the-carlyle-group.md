# The Carlyle Group

> A $477B global private equity/investment firm, sponsoring as a capital allocator and AI-infrastructure investor rather than a tool vendor.

- **Category:** vc-community
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 1987, Washington, D.C. (Verified)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.carlyle.com/ — no GitHub (not a product company)

## What they do
Carlyle is a global alternative-asset manager (private equity, credit, infrastructure) with ~$477B AUM as of Dec. 2025 and 1,800+ employees across 31 offices. It is not a developer-tool company; its AIEWF relevance is twofold: (1) it invests in and around AI — e.g., leading a Series B for SESAMm (AI-driven investment analytics using LLMs) — and increasingly frames AI infrastructure (data centers, power, compute) as an investment thesis; and (2) it is a documented internal adopter, building RAG/agentic pipelines for its own investment-research teams using tools like LlamaIndex's LlamaParse for parsing complex financial documents.

## Founders & origins
Founded in 1987 by five partners: William E. Conway Jr., Stephen L. Norris, David Rubenstein, Daniel A. D'Aniello, and Greg Rosenbaum. Conway, D'Aniello, and Rubenstein remained the firm's core long-term leadership; Rosenbaum left within the first year, Norris in 1995. (Verified)

## Evidence of real-world use
