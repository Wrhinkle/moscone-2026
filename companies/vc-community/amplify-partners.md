# Amplify Partners

> Early-stage VC firm that backs technical founders building developer tools, AI/ML infrastructure, and data infra — not an engineering product itself, but a recurring name behind the AI stack's plumbing.

## What they do
Amplify Partners is a seed/Series A venture firm ($1M–$10M checks, often ~$3M at seed) that invests specifically in technical founders across AI/ML, developer tools, data infrastructure, and cybersecurity, plus a new dedicated digital-biology fund. It's a capital and network provider, not a product an engineer would integrate — relevant to this repo as an investor whose portfolio signals which infra bets are gaining traction.

**Founders (Verified):** Sunil Dhaliwal founded the firm in 2012 after 15 years at Battery Ventures; Mike Dauber joined as co-building General Partner in 2014 (also ex-Battery). Other GPs include Lenny Pruss and Sarah Catanzaro.

**Funding (Verified):** Closed three new funds in June 2025 totaling $900M — Fund VI ($400M core), Fund VI Select ($300M, follow-on), and Amplify Bio I ($200M, digital biology).

## Evidence of real-world use
Portfolio includes Datadog, Fastly, Runway, Modal, Luma AI, LangChain, Temporal, and Chainguard; firm claims 14 unicorns, 5 IPOs, and 50 acquisitions across its history.

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Not a tool in the agent stack, but a bellwether: its bets (Modal for compute, LangChain for orchestration, Runway/Luma for generative models) track closely with the infra layers this landscape repo catalogs, useful for spotting which agent/infra startups are getting credible early backing.
