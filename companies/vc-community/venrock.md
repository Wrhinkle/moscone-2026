# Venrock

> A century-old-money, five-decade-old venture capital firm (the Rockefeller family's VC arm) that funds early-stage technology, healthcare, and AI companies.

- **Category:** vc-community
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 1969, formalized from Laurance S. Rockefeller's startup investing dating to the 1930s. Offices in Palo Alto, New York City, and Boston. (Verified — multiple sources incl. Wikipedia)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.venrock.com — no GitHub (not applicable; VC firm)

## What they do
Venrock is a traditional early-stage VC firm (Series A/B focus, reported check sizes $500K–$20M, ~$3B AUM) rather than a product or platform — nothing here to integrate technically. It invests across enterprise software, cybersecurity, fintech, digital health/biotech, and AI. Historically one of Silicon Valley's oldest firms, with early bets on Intel, Apple, and 3Com, and more recent exits including Nest Labs (acquired by Google, $3.2B) and Dollar Shave Club (acquired by Unilever, $1B). Reported total track record: 440+ portfolio companies, 125+ IPOs.

## Founders & origins
Founded by Laurance S. Rockefeller, formalizing family investing activity as Venrock Associates (a "Venture + Rockefeller" portmanteau), funded by Rockefeller family members and affiliated institutions (MoMA, Rockefeller University, Memorial Sloan-Kettering). (Verified via Wikipedia and multiple secondary sources.)

## Evidence of real-world use
As a VC firm, "usage" = deployed capital and exits. Verified/reported recent AI-adjacent portfolio activity includes leading a $13M seed round in Archetype AI (alongside Amazon's Industrial Innovation Fund and Hitachi Ventures) and backing Nightfall (ML-based cloud data-loss-prevention). Broader historical portfolio (Intel, Apple, Nest, Dollar Shave Club) is well documented but predates the current AI wave.
