## What they do
Gradient is a seed/pre-seed venture fund investing exclusively in AI startups — applications, agentic platforms, and AI-augmented real-world systems. Originally launched by Google in 2017 as a corporate venture arm (with technical mentorship and Google AI expert access as part of the pitch), it spun out in October 2025 to invest independently of Google's cap-table conflicts, closing a $220M fifth fund in 2026 to seed-stage AI founders (Verified — Wall Street Journal reporting via multiple secondary sources).

## Founders & origins
Managing partners Darian Shirazi and Zach Bratun-Glennon lead the firm post-spinout (Verified). Original Google program leadership not confirmed in sources reviewed.

## Funding
Firm manages ~$1.2B across five funds; fifth fund closed at $220M in 2026, average check size ~$3M for 10–15% ownership (Reported, secondary press — not independently cross-verified against a primary SEC filing).

## Evidence of real-world use
Portfolio includes ~250 companies; notable exits/investments cited include Lambda (GPU cloud), Streamlit (acquired by Snowflake), CentML (acquired by Nvidia), Writer, and Krea (Reported).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
As a funder rather than a tool, Gradient's relevance is ecosystem-level: it's writing seed checks specifically into agentic-platform and AI-infrastructure startups, making its portfolio a leading indicator of where agent tooling investment is heading.
