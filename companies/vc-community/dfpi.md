# DFPI (California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation)

> Not a startup — a California state regulator. Its AIEWF presence connects to its dual role policing AI-linked financial fraud and, as of 2026, overseeing new VC-industry diversity reporting rules.

- **Category:** vc-community
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** State agency; "Innovation" added to its name/mandate in 2020 (Verified, ca.gov)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://dfpi.ca.gov — n/a (government agency, no product/repo)

## What they do
The DFPI is California's financial-services regulator (successor to the Department of Business Oversight), covering lending, money transmission, crypto, and investment advice licensing in the state. Two threads make it relevant to an AI/VC audience: (1) it actively investigates and issues desist-and-refrain orders against AI-linked investment fraud (e.g., "AI crypto trading" high-yield-investment scams); and (2) under California's 2026 Fair Investment Practices by Venture Capital Companies law (FIPVCC), the DFPI became the regulator collecting mandatory founder-demographic disclosures from VC funds with a California nexus — though as of March 2026 the DFPI suspended enforcement/reporting pending formal rulemaking (Verified, multiple law-firm client alerts). This is why it fits a "vc-community" bucket: it now sits directly inside VC fund compliance workflows, not just consumer protection.

Verified regulatory actions exist (desist-and-refrain orders against AI-investment-fraud schemes) and the VCC reporting program is a live (if currently paused) compliance requirement for VC funds — this is a real regulatory relationship for any AI-focused fund or portfolio company operating in/with California, not a vendor relationship.
