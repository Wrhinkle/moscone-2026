## What they do (relevant slice)
For this repo, "UC Berkeley" means its AI research output, not an investment vehicle. Berkeley AI Research (BAIR) and the Sky Computing Lab (co-directed by Prof. Joseph Gonzalez) produced Gorilla (a fine-tuned LLaMA model for API/tool-calling, plus the Berkeley Function-Calling Leaderboard), Vicuña, Chatbot Arena/LMSYS, and vLLM — a widely deployed high-throughput LLM serving engine (near-zero-waste KV cache, 2-4x throughput gains). Berkeley also announced an "AI Center of Excellence" (Dec. 2024). No funding/founders section applies — this is a university, not a company.

## Evidence of real-world use
vLLM is one of the most widely adopted open-source LLM inference servers in production today; Gorilla/BFCL helped catalyze function-calling/tool-use benchmarking industry-wide (Verified — project pages, press coverage).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Directly foundational: tool-calling (Gorilla/BFCL), model serving (vLLM), and model evaluation via head-to-head arenas (Chatbot Arena) are core building blocks referenced across this repo's inference and evals research areas.
