# Navan

> Corporate travel-and-expense platform (formerly TripActions) that has built an agentic AI layer, Navan Cognition, on top of its own booking/expense workflows.

- **Category:** back-office-automation
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2015, Palo Alto, CA (as TripActions; rebranded Navan in 2023) — Verified
- **Website / GitHub:** https://navan.com

## What they do
Navan is a T&E (travel and expense) SaaS platform: booking flights/hotels, corporate cards, expense reports, and reconciliation, sold to mid-market and enterprise finance/ops teams. In 2025 it launched **Navan Cognition**, a multi-agent framework with built-in supervisor agents, API-level validation, and domain-specific reasoning layers designed to catch hallucinations before they reach a customer — productized first inside Navan's own support/travel-agent chatbot ("Ava") and now offered as a platform for other companies to build reliability-critical AI agents. Navan went public on Nasdaq (NAVN) in October 2025.

## Founders & funding
Co-founded by Ariel Cohen and Ilan Twig (also CTO). Raised ~$1.58B total across 15 rounds pre-IPO (Series G at $9.2B valuation, 2022); investors include a16z, Coatue, Goldman Sachs, Lightspeed. Verified via Crunchbase/press.

## Evidence of real-world use
Ava, Navan's own agent, reportedly handled two years of production travel-support chats without a "critical hallucination," per Navan and external commentary from a Columbia neuroscience professor cited in press coverage (Reported, single-narrative source — Navan's own case study).

**Naming note:** a separate, unrelated "Navan.ai" (autonomous coding/computer-vision tooling) also exists online; the AIEWF sponsor link resolves to navan.com, confirming this profile is the travel/expense company — Verified via sponsor page fetch.

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Direct case study in **reliability/guardrails for production agents** — multi-agent supervision, API validation, hallucination containment — relevant to evals/observability and agent-orchestration tracks in this repo.
