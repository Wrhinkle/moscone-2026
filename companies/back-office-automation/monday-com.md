# monday.com

> Publicly traded work-management platform (NASDAQ: MNDY) that has repositioned itself in 2026 as an "AI Work Platform" combining human workflows with configurable AI agents.

- **Category:** back-office-automation
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2012, Tel Aviv, Israel (Verified)
- **Website:** https://monday.com

## What they do
monday.com is a no-code work-management/CRM/project-tracking platform; the engineer-relevant shift is its 2026 relaunch as an "AI Work Platform" where non-technical users configure, deploy, and direct native AI agents alongside human teammates. These agents are marketed to draft campaigns, qualify leads, close support tickets, onboard new hires, and process purchase requests — squarely back-office/ops automation — under human supervision, with consumption-based pricing layered on top of the existing SaaS model.

- **Founders/funding:** Co-founded by Roy Mann and Eran Zinman (Verified, still co-CEOs); IPO'd 2021. Not currently fundraising — publicly traded, reported ~$1.23B trailing revenue, and it has launched its own $200M "Monday Ventures" fund to invest in AI tooling (Reported).

## Evidence of real-world use
This is a live product launch (announced via investor-relations release and Businesswire, March 2026), not a lab prototype — it ships to monday.com's existing large paying customer base. Independent verification of agent effectiveness/adoption numbers was not found; treat efficacy claims as vendor-reported pending third-party case studies.

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Directly relevant to this repo's back-office-automation thesis: a mainstream SaaS incumbent turning its workflow graph into an agent-deployment surface for non-engineers is a strong signal for where "configure, don't code" agent tooling is headed, and a useful comparator for the Job Packet Readiness Auditor demo concept.

## Use cases & considerations
Use cases: lead qualification, support-ticket resolution, HR onboarding, purchase-request processing — all inside monday's existing board/workflow model. Considerations: platform lock-in (agents are monday-native, not portable), consumption pricing on top of seat pricing raises total cost, and it competes with Asana, ClickUp, and now-agent-enabled Notion/Airtable in the same space. Open question: how these agents' reliability/guardrails compare to purpose-built agent frameworks.
