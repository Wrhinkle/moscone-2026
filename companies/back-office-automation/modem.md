# Modem

> An AI-native product-management layer that turns customer feedback, support tickets, and PR/issue activity into a live backlog an AI teammate helps you act on.

- **Category:** back-office-automation
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** silver
- **Founded:** 2024 (Verified — pre-seed announcement); San Francisco/remote (Reported)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://modem.dev

## What they do
Modem (not to be confused with Modal, the compute platform, or Modular) is a "product execution" tool: it ingests customer feedback, support conversations, and issue/PR metadata from your existing stack, then uses an AI agent to synthesize what users are asking for, keep a prioritized backlog current, and hand off well-scoped work — including delegating multi-step coding tasks directly to coding agents like Devin or Claude Code, enriched with the feedback context it has collected. In engineer terms: it's a context-aggregation and triage layer sitting between your support/feedback tools and your issue tracker/coding agents, closing the loop from "user complained" to "agent shipped a fix."

## Founders & origins
Co-founded by Ben Vinegar (CEO, ex-founding engineer/VP Engineering at Sentry) and Mike Clarke (CTO, ex-CPO at Treasury Prime), with early team members from Sentry, Vercel, Trunk, and Sprig (Verified via company site and BetaKit).

## Funding
Closed a $4.4M USD pre-seed round led by Accel, with Montreal's Inovia Capital participating; angel backers include Sentry co-founders David Cramer and Chris Jennings, Cohere co-founder Ivan Zhang, Flask creator Armin Ronacher, and Bootstrap creator Jacob Thornton (Verified — BetaKit, company site).

## Evidence of real-world use
SOC 2 certified and actively hiring; backed by operator-angels from Sentry, Vercel, and Cohere who are plausible early users, but no named customer logos or public case studies were found.

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Sits in the "back-office/SDLC" layer — using an agent to bridge unstructured customer signal and structured engineering work, then delegating execution to existing coding agents rather than building its own.
