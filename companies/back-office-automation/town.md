# Town

> A personal AI assistant that plugs into your inbox, calendar, and other daily tools and acts proactively — accumulated personal context, not a better model, is the pitch.

- **Category:** back-office-automation
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** late 2024, San Francisco (Reported)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.town.com/ — no GitHub (consumer/prosumer product, not OSS)

## What they do
Town is a personal AI assistant that connects to the tools a user already works in — inbox, calendar, Slack, docs — and learns their voice, relationships, and priorities to suggest and take actions proactively rather than waiting for prompts. Signup requires connecting email/calendar so the system can build a working model of the user. The company reportedly started narrower — tax-advisory services for small businesses — before widening into a general proactive assistant by mid-2026. Disambiguation note: this is Town, Inc. (town.com), distinct from the unrelated open-source "AI Town" simulation project (a16z-infra/ai-town on GitHub).

## Founders & origins
Co-founded by Jean-Denis Grèze (CEO; former CTO of Plaid, where he scaled engineering from 20 to 350+ people) and Tony Vincent (CPO; former Director of Applied AI at Google, previously Head of Design at Dropbox — the two were colleagues/friends from Dropbox). (Verified — corroborated across Fortune, The Information, Crunchbase)

## Funding
$18M seed led by First Round Capital (~March 2025, when framed around tax services), followed by a $55M Series A led by Andreessen Horowitz with Forerunner Ventures, First Round, Alt Capital, and Conviction (reported June 2026, coinciding with the pivot to a general personal assistant). (Reported)

## Evidence of real-world use
Coverage (Fortune, GlobeNewswire, Yahoo Finance/TechTimes) confirms the funding and product framing; no named enterprise customers or usage metrics were found in public sources — evidence so far is investor-backing and press, not documented deployments. (Unverified beyond funding/press)

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
A direct back-office-automation example: an agent with persistent memory/context across a user's real tool surface (email, calendar, docs) making autonomous suggestions — relevant to memory architecture and proactive-agent design patterns in this repo's agentic stack.
