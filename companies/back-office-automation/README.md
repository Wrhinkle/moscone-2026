# Back-Office Automation

Document AI and extraction, finance and procurement automation, agreements/contracts, vertical operations AI (healthcare, insurance, property), internal tools, and work management. This is the category where agents meet paperwork: the real-world problem is that most companies' operational state lives in unstructured artifacts (invoices, contracts, claim files, faxes, tickets, emails) and in the systems of record (ERP, CLM, ITSM, PMS) that humans manually reconcile against them. Every company here sells some version of the same loop: ingest messy input → extract structured, typed data → act inside a system of record → keep a human approval gate and an audit trail. The differences are where in that loop they sit and how much of it they own.

## How to read this category

Three profiles carry the most weight. **[Reducto](reducto.md)** (the category's only platinum sponsor) defines the ingestion layer (if an agent can't read the PDF, nothing downstream matters), and its profile doubles as a map of the parse/extract competitive field. **[Atlassian](atlassian.md)** is the archetype of the incumbent play: a system of record (Jira/Confluence) turning itself into both an agent-callable tool surface (via MCP) and an agent-hosting platform (Rovo). **[Anterior](anterior.md)** is the cleanest vertical case study: regulated document intake, policy-as-rules, evidence-cited verdicts, mandatory human review. Every other regulated-vertical company here is a variation on that pattern.

The axis that differentiates the rest is **API layer vs. owned workflow**:

- **Developer-facing extraction APIs** you build with: Reducto, [Extend](extend.md), [Bem](bem.md). All three sit at the same layer and compete on accuracy, workflow orchestration, and governance respectively.
- **Vertical operators** that own the whole workflow in a regulated domain: Anterior and [Hippocratic AI](hippocratic-ai.md) (healthcare), [Wisedocs](wisedocs.md) (insurance claims), [EliseAI](eliseai.md) (rental housing), [Long Lake](long-lake.md) (which skips software sales entirely and just buys the HOA-management businesses).
- **Horizontal incumbents bolting agents onto their moat**: Atlassian, [ServiceNow](servicenow.md), [Docusign](docusign.md), [Ironclad](ironclad.md), [Box](box.md), [Notion](notion.md), [monday.com](monday-com.md), [Navan](navan.md). The data/workflow graph is the asset; agents are the new interface.
- **The glue and consoles**: [Retool](retool.md) (human-review UIs and hourly-priced agents), [Ravenna](ravenna.md) (Slack-native ITSM), [Auditoria AI](auditoria-ai.md) (CFO-suite agents over the ERP), [Modem](modem.md) (feedback-to-backlog triage), [Town](town.md) (personal proactive assistant), [InsightSciences.ai](insightsciences-ai.md) (synthetic research personas; the thinnest profile here, usage unverified).

**Start here:** Reducto → Anterior → Atlassian, then Retool if you're building (it's the human-in-the-loop half most agent stacks are missing) and Auditoria if you care about governance design ("Governed Autonomy" is the most explicit production articulation of policy-scoped agent authority in this folder).

## Related papers

The papers most load-bearing for this category, in rough order of how often the profiles above lean on them:

- **[Governance by Construction for Generalist Agents](../../papers/tool-use-governance/governance-by-construction-for-generalist-agents.md)**: the single most-cited paper in this folder. Auditoria's Governed Autonomy, Ironclad's playbook-fenced redlining, Anterior's mandatory clinician review, Docusign's signature authorization boundaries, and Box/Notion/Atlassian's permission-scoped MCP surfaces are all production instances of its argument.
- **[The Evolution of Tool Use in LLM Agents: From Single-Tool Call to Multi-Tool Orchestration](../../papers/tool-use-governance/the-evolution-of-tool-use-in-llm-agents-from-single-tool-ca.md)**: the frame for every "agent acting on a system of record" story here. Jira/Docusign/Notion as tool targets, Auditoria's AP/AR suites, Bem's composable typed functions, Retool's connector-as-tool-inventory bet.
- **[CaMeLs Can Use Computers Too: System-level Security for Computer Use Agents](../../papers/tool-use-governance/camels-can-use-computers-too-system-level-security-for-comp.md)**: the security companion to the governance paper; directly invoked by Auditoria (adversarial vendor emails) and Docusign (legally binding actions).
- **[Memory for Autonomous LLM Agents: Mechanisms, Architectures, and Evaluation](../../papers/memory-research-agents/memory-for-autonomous-llm-agents-mechanisms-architectures.md)**: long-horizon back-office state. Contract obligations/renewals (Docusign), collections behavior histories (Auditoria), multi-month renter journeys (EliseAI), medical chronologies (Wisedocs), workspace-as-memory (Notion).
- **[Are We Ready For An Agent-Native Memory System?](../../papers/memory-research-agents/are-we-ready-for-an-agent-native-memory-system.md)**: Notion's pages-and-databases-as-agent-memory is a shipping counterproposal to the opaque vector store.
- **[Do Agents Need Semantic Metadata? A Comparative Study in Agentic Data Retrieval](../../papers/memory-research-agents/do-agents-need-semantic-metadata-a-comparative-study-in-age.md)**: why the extraction layer matters. - **[Reproducible, Explainable, and Effective Evaluations of Agentic AI for Software Engineering](../../papers/swe-agents/reproducible-explainable-and-effective-evaluations-of-agen.md)**: the eval-loop pattern behind Reducto's self-checking OCR, Bem's golden-dataset regression testing, Extend's Composer agent, and Retool's "hours of agent work" metric.

## Open questions the category hasn't answered

1. **Does the extraction layer survive frontier models?** Reducto, Extend, and Bem all face the same durability question their own profiles raise: as multimodal models get "good enough" at raw PDF reading, is workflow/eval/governance tooling enough moat, or does the layer collapse into the model?
2. **Incumbent agents vs. thin custom agents.** Docusign's profile poses it directly: does an incumbent's packaged agent beat a custom agent calling the incumbent's plain APIs? The same question hangs over Rovo, Iris, ServiceNow's Agent Studio, and monday.com's agents; none has independent efficacy evidence yet.
3. **How much is actually agentic?** Auditoria's profile asks openly whether the suites are LLM-agentic or rules/RPA underneath; most enterprise vendors here don't disclose. Vendor-reported outcome metrics (Long Lake's productivity gains, Hippocratic's "115M interactions, no safety issues," monday.com's agent claims) are almost all unaudited.
4. **Who owns the human-review surface?** Every credible deployment in this folder has an approval gate, but the console lives in different places: Retool apps, Notion databases, JSM queues, Bem's review inbox, Wisedocs' clinician layer. No standard has emerged for the escalation/audit UI half of agent stacks.
5. **Regulatory exposure as both moat and risk.** Prior auth (Anterior) is under CMS pressure, fair-housing liability shadows leasing agents (EliseAI's sector), and "$9/hr AI nurses" (Hippocratic) draws union pushback. Compliance builds the moat and can also shrink the market.
6. **Unverified corners.** InsightSciences.ai has no publicly confirmable customers or funding, and Long Lake's founder identities and exact raise are undisclosed; both are indexed here with that caveat.
