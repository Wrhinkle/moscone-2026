# Ravenna

> An AI-native ITSM/helpdesk that lives inside Slack, classifying and auto-resolving internal IT/HR/RevOps tickets instead of routing them to a queue.

- **Category:** back-office-automation
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** bronze
- **Founded:** 2024 (started "nine months" before an April 2025 raise announcement — Reported), Seattle, WA (Verified — GeekWire, Madrona)
- **Website:** ravenna.ai

## What they do
Ravenna is an "AI-native ITSM" platform built Slack-first: employees file IT/HR/business-ops requests directly in Slack, and Ravenna classifies the request, surfaces relevant internal knowledge, and automates resolution of common workflows — tickets are created, triaged, and closed without leaving Slack. It's positioned explicitly against both legacy ITSM (Jira Service Management, Zendesk) and simple AI chatbot bolt-ons ("go beyond AI agents and chatbots"), claiming up to a 70% cut in ticket volume through automation rather than mere deflection.

## Founders & funding
Co-founders Taylor Halliday (CEO, ex-director of engineering/AI at Zapier) and Kevin Coleman (ex-AWS go-to-market) — previously built Seattle consultancy Mesh Studio together and went through YC in 2016 (Verified — GeekWire). $15M pre-seed + seed from Madrona and Khosla Ventures plus angels (Verified — company blog, Madrona).

## Evidence of real-world use
Design partners named include Futureverse, Homebase, and Zapier, per Madrona's investment writeup (Reported, single source; standard "design partner" framing rather than independently documented case studies).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
A concrete back-office-automation deployment of agentic AI: request classification, knowledge retrieval, and workflow execution applied to internal support rather than customer-facing use — relevant to any research on agent reliability in operational (non-coding) domains.
