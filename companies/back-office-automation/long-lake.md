# Long Lake

> A General Catalyst-incubated "AI roll-up": Long Lake acquires back-office service businesses (starting with HOA/community management) and rebuilds their operations around AI tooling rather than building AI-native software from scratch.

- **Category:** back-office-automation
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2023, incubated inside General Catalyst (Verified — General Catalyst, TechCrunch)
- **Website:** https://llmh.com (Long Lake Management Holdings) · https://www.generalcatalyst.com/companies/long-lake

## Disambiguation note
"Long Lake" is a generic name with many unrelated hits (real-estate listings, unrelated capital-management LLCs). Based on it appearing as a confirmed AIEWF 2026 supporting-tier sponsor and its fit with the back-office-automation category, this profile covers **Long Lake Management Holdings**, the General Catalyst-backed AI-enabled roll-up — the only "Long Lake" plausibly relevant to an AI engineering conference among candidates found.

## What they do
Rather than selling software, Long Lake buys operating businesses in "ho-hum," thin-margin services verticals (its first deals were HOA/community management companies, since expanded to Residential, Business, and Infrastructure Services) and then deploys AI tooling directly into those operations — e.g., AI-generated HOA board meeting presentations, AI-assisted sales motions. This is the "AI-enabled roll-up" model other General Catalyst-backed vehicles (and Khosla Ventures) are also pursuing: PE-style acquisition plus in-house AI/automation engineering as the value-creation lever, versus a traditional SaaS go-to-market.

## Founders & funding
Incubated by General Catalyst starting 2023; specific individual founder names were not found in public sources (Unverified). Raised roughly $670M–$1.28B across investors including General Catalyst, Alpha Wave Global, Thrive Capital, and Lightscape Partners depending on source/date (Reported — figures vary by outlet, treat as directional).

## Evidence of real-world use
Long Lake has acquired 18 businesses to date and reports reaching $100M in EBITDA in under two years, with 25–30% productivity gains in HOA management teams and a reported 10x increase in new-customer sales pipeline from AI-powered sales tooling (Reported — General Catalyst, industry press; largely company/investor-sourced claims, not independently audited).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
A live test of "does AI actually change P&L in a boring back-office business" — directly relevant to back-office-automation and enterprise-adopters themes at AIEWF, and a useful counterpoint to pure dev-tooling narratives: the AI value here is in operations automation (documents, scheduling, sales workflows) layered onto acquired legacy businesses.
