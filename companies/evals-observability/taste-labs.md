# Taste Labs

> Selling "taste" as infrastructure: preference data, rubrics, and eval environments so AI models stop producing generic, on-model-but-off-brand slop.

- **Category:** evals-observability
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2025, disclosed from stealth 2026 (Reported)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://tastelabs.com/ (also referenced as tastelabs.ai) — no public GitHub found

## What they do
Taste Labs builds a data/infrastructure layer aimed at the subjective-quality gap in generative AI — what shows up in the industry as "AI slop." Two customer motions: (1) supplying frontier AI labs with preference datasets, rubrics, and evaluation environments used to grade and improve the subjective quality (taste, style, on-brand-ness) of model outputs — the same category of work as RLHF/eval-environment vendors but focused on aesthetic/qualitative judgment rather than correctness; and (2) giving application-layer companies context and verification tooling so their agents produce output that matches a brand's voice/style rather than generic model defaults. In engineer terms: a rubric-and-preference-data provider for evals that target "does this look/sound right" rather than "is this factually correct."

## Founders & origins
Founded by Thais Castello Branco, former growth lead at Exa (a Benchmark-backed AI search startup); joined the Latitud Fellowship in 2024. Founding designer has ~15 years as a VP of Design, serving as in-house "arbiter of taste." (Reported)

## Funding
$18.5M seed, co-led by Amplify Partners and CRV. (Reported)

## Evidence of real-world use
Public coverage (Latitud, Amplify Partners' own investment post, The Source Code, Dealroom) describes the launch and thesis but no named enterprise customers or case studies were found in public sources at time of writing. (Unverified beyond investor/press launch coverage)

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Directly touches evals: rubric and preference-data design for subjective/qualitative grading is an underserved slice of the eval stack most agentic pipelines skip in favor of correctness-only checks. Worth watching alongside RLHF vendors (Surge AI) and eval platforms in this same category.
