# Relai (RELAI, relai.ai)

> A verifiable continual-learning platform that turns AI agents' failures, traces, evals and human feedback into ongoing improvements to prompts, workflows, tools and memory.

- **Website:** https://relai.ai

## What they do

Relai ingests an agent's failure cases, execution traces, evaluation scores and human feedback, and uses them as an "online, in-loop regression control" — continuously re-optimizing prompts, workflows, tool selection and contextual memory, then validating each change against prior environments before it ships, rather than only checking quality after deployment (Verified — company blog, SiliconANGLE). In effect it's a feedback-loop/eval layer sitting on top of a deployed agent stack, aimed at closing the gap between offline eval scores and production reliability.

## Founders & funding

Founded by **Soheil Feizi**, associate computer-science professor at the University of Maryland (MIT PhD, Presidential Early Career Award recipient) (Verified). **$6.9M** total: $5.4M pre-seed led by **.406 Ventures** (with AI Tinkerers Fund) plus $1.5M pre-pre-seed from Non sibi Ventures and TEDCO, announced June 2026 (Verified — SiliconANGLE, company blog).

## Evidence of real-world use

Company-reported early deployments: a financial-services agent's validation score rose from 39% to 80%, a healthcare proof-of-concept from 62% to 96% (Reported — vendor blog/press, unaudited, no named customers).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering

Sits in the evals/observability + continual-learning niche of the agent stack — relevant to work on production eval loops, regression-safe prompt/tool optimization, and closing the offline-eval-to-production gap.
