# Jellyfish

> An "engineering intelligence" platform that pulls signal out of Git, Jira, CI/CD, and (now) AI coding-tool telemetry to give engineering leaders a dashboard on where time and code actually go.

- **Category:** swe-infrastructure
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2017, Boston, MA (Verified)
- **Website:** https://jellyfish.co

## What they do
Jellyfish ingests data from source control, project trackers, and CI systems and maps engineering work to business initiatives — allocation of effort, delivery velocity, DORA metrics, and cost. Its newer surface, **Jellyfish AI Impact**, extends this to measure AI coding-tool adoption and ROI: it integrates with GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code, Amazon Q, Gemini Code Assist, Windsurf, and CodeRabbit, tracking metrics like "AI Code Percentage" (share of merged code that's AI-assisted) and "Autonomous Agent Activity" (share of PRs autonomously generated by agents) against quality and speed outcomes (Verified — jellyfish.co product pages).

## Founders & funding
Founded by Andrew Lau (CEO), Philip Braden, and David Gourley, former colleagues at Endeca (acquired by Oracle for ~$1B). Raised ~$114M total across Series A–C, including a $71M Series C led by Accel, Insight Partners, and Tiger Global (Verified — Crunchbase, TechCrunch, Insight Partners).

## Evidence of real-world use
Jellyfish reports its AI benchmark study analyzed 20M+ pull requests and found 90% of engineering teams now use AI coding tools (up from 61% a year prior), with 62% reporting ≥25% productivity gains (Reported — Jellyfish newsroom/blog, self-published research).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Directly relevant to measuring the "agentic SDLC" — one of AIEWF's own 2026 track themes. Jellyfish is a case study in evals/observability applied not to model outputs but to engineering-org outcomes from agent adoption.
