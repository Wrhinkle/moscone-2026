# Moonlake AI

> An applied-research lab building generative "world models" — text/image/point-cloud-to-interactive-3D-scene reasoning models — for games, robotics, and physical-AI simulation.

- **Category:** models-inference
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2025, San Francisco, CA (Reported — press coverage; not independently cross-checked against a registry)
- **Website:** https://moonlakeai.com/

## What they do
Moonlake trains reasoning/generative models that turn a natural-language description (or image/point-cloud/gaussian-splat input) into a working interactive 3D scene or simulation environment, including physics, articulation, and deformable objects, within minutes — positioning itself as "world/asset infrastructure for physical AI" rather than a text-LLM API provider. Note: this puts them closer to generative-world-model / simulation research than to the LLM-inference tooling the "models-inference" category usually implies — worth flagging for anyone expecting an inference-serving vendor.

- **Founders/funding:** Founded by Fan-Yun Sun (ex-Stanford AI Lab/NVIDIA) and Sharon Lee (Verified via multiple press sources). Raised $28M seed (Reported) from AIX Ventures, Threshold, NVIDIA Ventures, and individual backers including Naval Ravikant, Ian Goodfellow, and Steve Chen.

## Evidence of real-world use
Company launched from stealth in October 2025 with press coverage (Forbes, TechStartups) but no named enterprise customers or case studies found; product use cases described (UGC game worlds, robotics/embodied-AI training sims, film/animation 3D environments) are stated intent, not documented deployments (Unverified for actual usage).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Adjacent rather than core: relevant to agent training via simulation/RL environments and robotics/embodied-agent evaluation, less directly to LLM tool-calling or SDLC-agent work covered elsewhere in this repo.
