# Prime Intellect

> Decentralized compute and training infrastructure company: aggregates global GPU supply and provides open frameworks/hubs for distributed pretraining and RL on frontier-scale models.

- **Category:** models-inference
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2023, San Francisco, CA — Verified
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.primeintellect.ai · https://github.com/PrimeIntellect-ai

## What they do
Prime Intellect runs a peer-to-peer/decentralized compute marketplace and builds open-source frameworks (OpenDiLoCo, PRIME) for training large models across unreliable, globally distributed, low-bandwidth nodes. They've shipped a series of fully open training runs — INTELLECT-1 (10B, Llama-3 arch), INTELLECT-2 (32B RL), and INTELLECT-3 (100B+ MoE, RL-trained) — plus the **Environments Hub**, an open marketplace for RL training/eval environments, and `prime-rl`/`verifiers`, their open agentic-RL training and evals libraries.

## Founders & funding
Founded by Vincent Weisser (ex-VitaDAO, Molecule) and Johannes Hagemann (built distributed-training frameworks at Aleph Alpha, NeurIPS 2023 best-paper award) — Verified. Raised $5.5M seed (2024, Distributed Global/CoinFund) then $15M Series A (Feb 2025, led by Founders Fund; angels include Andrej Karpathy, Clem Delangue, Tri Dao) — ~$70.4M total reported.

## Evidence of real-world use
Environments Hub beta drew contributions/recognition from Arcee AI, Hud.so, WhyPhy Labs, and Groq; PRIME/OpenDiLoCo/prime-rl are public GitHub repos with active community use, not just marketing claims.

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Directly relevant to **models/inference and evals** tracks: open RL environments and agentic RL training infra are foundational to building and evaluating tool-using agents, and their decentralized-compute thesis is an alternative to hyperscaler-locked training.
