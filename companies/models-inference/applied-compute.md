# Applied Compute

> Cloud platform for training, serving, and continuously improving custom "Specific Intelligence" models/agents on a company's own proprietary data, rather than relying on general-purpose foundation models.

- **Category:** models-inference
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** Early 2025, San Francisco (**Verified**)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.appliedcompute.com — no public GitHub found

## What they do
Applied Compute offers three integrated capabilities: **Train** (infra for custom/RL-based model training on proprietary data optimized to a company's own eval metrics), **Serve** (production inference, serverless or in-VPC), and **Improve** (continuous post-deployment tuning via online RL on real production feedback). It supports models from 1B to 1T+ parameters and explicitly positions against "generic" foundation-model usage, pitching customer-owned, domain-tuned agents ("Specific Intelligence") as the fix for smart-but-not-useful enterprise AI.

**Founders (Verified):** Rhythm Garg, Linden Li, and Yash Patil (CEO) — ex-OpenAI researchers; Patil worked on Codex, Garg on o1-era reasoning RL, Li on RL training infrastructure.

**Funding (Verified, note discrepancy):** Launched with $20M then $80M (total $160M cited), valuing the company at ~$1.3B; sources conflict on the lead of the $80M round — some (SiliconANGLE, Pulse2) cite Benchmark/Sequoia/Lux/Elad Gil, while Applied Compute's own site and later coverage (Phemex, Dealroom) cite Kleiner Perkins as lead of an April 2026 round. Likely two distinct rounds reported inconsistently across outlets — flagged for follow-up.

## Evidence of real-world use
Named customers/testimonials on its own site: Cognition, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Harvey, DoorDash, Handshake, NTT Data, Mercor, and others (self-reported logos, not independently verified case studies).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Sits at the model-training/fine-tuning and inference layer of the agent stack — relevant to RL-based agent post-training, eval-driven optimization, and enterprise "own your model" strategies discussed alongside evals/observability work in this repo.
