# AIDAChip

> An "alignment OS" for chip design teams — AI copilots for semiconductor IP development wired together with shared team context instead of working in per-engineer silos.

- **Category:** models-inference
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** Not found in public sources (recent; Santa Clara, CA) (**Unverified**)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://www.aidachip.com — no public GitHub found

## What they do
AIDAChip builds AI-native tooling for semiconductor engineering teams rather than individual chip designers: automation for PLL design, SerDes IP development, analog design assistance, and digital verification, with claimed effects like faster design cycles, verification coverage boosts, Fmax gains, and power reduction. Its differentiator, per the company, is "multiplayer" workflow — shared context across roles and handoffs (design, verification, analog) rather than isolated per-engineer AI copilots. Note this is model-adjacent tooling for hardware design, not a chip vendor itself — an odd fit for a "models-inference" bucket versus a hardware-design-tools one, worth flagging for placement review.

**Founders (Reported):** Khaled Alashmouny, Founder & CEO — 13 years at Apple as a Silicon/AMS IC design manager, PhD/MS from University of Michigan (EECS).

**Funding:** Not found in public sources — Crunchbase profile exists but funding figures are not disclosed there.

## Evidence of real-world use
Company claims customers include "public companies" seeing measurable PPA/verification gains, but no named customers or case studies were found — treat as unverified marketing claims, not confirmed adoption.

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Represents agentic AI applied to a non-software engineering discipline (EDA/silicon design) — relevant as a case study in domain-specific agent teams and shared-context/multi-agent handoff patterns, rather than core LLM inference infrastructure.
