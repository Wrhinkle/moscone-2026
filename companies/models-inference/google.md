# Google

> The frontier-model and inference-platform giant behind Gemini, DeepMind, Vertex AI, and (as of 2026) an agent-first dev platform, Antigravity.

- **Category:** models-inference
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 1998, Menlo Park/Mountain View, CA (Verified; Alphabet subsidiary since 2015)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://ai.google.dev · https://cloud.google.com/vertex-ai · https://github.com/google

## What they do
For AI engineers, "Google" here means the Gemini/DeepMind model-and-inference stack: the Gemini API and Google AI Studio for prototyping, Vertex AI for enterprise-grade inference (SLAs, provisioned throughput, VPC controls), and — new in 2026 — Gemini 3.5 (Flash and Pro variants) plus Gemini Omni, a multimodal model taking video/any-input. At I/O 2026 Google shipped **Managed Agents in the Gemini API** (spin up a tool-using, code-executing agent in an isolated Linux sandbox with one API call, built on the Antigravity harness) and the **Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform**, consolidating Vertex AI into a build/scale/govern platform for agents. **Antigravity** is Google's new agent-first IDE/dev platform for taking a prompt to a production app, including a 2.0 desktop app. Pricing is usage-based per token (e.g., Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite ~$0.10/$0.40 per M input/output tokens; 2.5 Pro from $1.25/$10.00 per M, with long-context surcharges past 200K tokens) (Verified, Google docs).

## Founders & origins
Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin as a Stanford PhD project; became an Alphabet subsidiary in 2015. Publicly traded (IPO 2004) — not applicable for "funding rounds" (Verified).

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Core model/inference layer for much of the agent stack — model choice, managed-agent sandboxes, tool-calling, and now a competing agent-native IDE (Antigravity) that overlaps with Claude Code, Cursor, and Copilot in the SDLC-agent space.
