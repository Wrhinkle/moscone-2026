# Elorian

> An early-stage foundation-model lab building AI that reasons natively over visual/spatial input rather than converting images to text first.

- **Category:** models-inference
- **AIEWF 2026 tier:** supporting
- **Founded:** 2025, Palo Alto, CA (Reported — Bloomberg, Pulse2)
- **Website / GitHub:** https://elorian.ai/ (no public GitHub/API found)

## What they do
Elorian is a multimodal reasoning research lab (confirmed via elorian.ai; not to be confused with unrelated companies of similar name — searches consistently returned this one entity, so no disambiguation issue found). They're building foundation models that process visual input (images, spatial/design/physical data) as a first-class reasoning modality — not just OCR/caption-then-reason — targeting robotics, aerospace, medicine, and manufacturing. As of research date the site shows a research narrative and team page but no released product, API, or pricing — this is a pre-product frontier-model lab.

## Founders & origins
Co-founded by Andrew Dai, a ~14-year veteran of Google Brain/DeepMind (Verified — Bloomberg). Other co-founders described as former DeepMind researchers with backgrounds in pretraining, data, and vision modeling (Reported — company site, not individually named in press found).

## Funding
$55M seed round at a $300M valuation, backed by Menlo Ventures, Altimeter, Striker Ventures, and 49 Palms, with individual participation reported from Jeff Dean (Verified — multiple outlets: Bloomberg, VentureBurn, Pulse2, CornerForAI).

## Evidence of real-world use
None yet publicly documented — no named customers, no API/pricing page, no GitHub. Coverage describes "early commercial pilots planned" in industrial/factory automation, which is a forward-looking claim, not verified deployment.

## Relevance to agentic AI engineering
Relevant to the models/inference layer of an agent stack for agents needing native visual-spatial reasoning (robotics/physical-world agents, design/CAD copilot use cases) rather than today's text-first tool-calling agents. Worth watching, not yet evaluable — no public API.
